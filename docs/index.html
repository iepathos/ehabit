Freelancing Advice

Always try to improve your craft, whatever that may be. For me, it's programming. I view every job as an opportunity to better master programming, automation and systems architecture. Even the most tedious jobs I've done have been a positive learning experience for me because of this.

I've found a lot of clients will post smaller jobs as a way to test for good freelancers for larger projects. If you see a small task posted by a client with a lot of jobs then it could be worth doing a really good job on that small task to get at the bigger jobs from the client.

Try to get positive client feedback from every job if possible. Clients can be demanding sometimes but try to deliver what they want and if they are too much don't take future jobs from them. Remember you took a particular task but meeting your clients needs and expectations is the true task underlying every job.
